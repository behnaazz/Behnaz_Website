real *sholeh zard* takes hours, requires patience, and is rightfully one of
the most beloved persian desserts. this is not that. this is what i make on a
tuesday when it's grey outside and i need something warm and slightly fancy
in half an hour.

## ingredients

- 100 g short-grain rice (arborio works)
- 700 ml whole milk
- 100 ml double cream
- 3 tbsp sugar (or to taste — i use 2)
- a pinch of salt
- a generous pinch of saffron threads (~10 strands)
- 1 tsp rosewater
- 1/2 tsp cardamom, freshly ground
- to top: slivered pistachios, dried rose petals if you have them

## method

1. **bloom the saffron.** crush the threads between your fingers into a small
   bowl. pour over 2 tbsp of just-boiled water. leave to steep while you do
   everything else — it should turn deep amber.
2. **rinse the rice** until the water runs almost clear. drain.
3. in a heavy-bottomed pot, combine rice, milk, cream, sugar, and salt. bring
   to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat, stirring often.
4. **simmer for 20–25 min**, stirring lazily but regularly. you want the rice
   completely soft and the mixture creamy — almost like risotto, but sweeter
   and slacker.
5. once the rice is tender, stir in the **bloomed saffron, rosewater, and
   cardamom**. cook 2 more minutes.
6. ladle into small bowls. top with pistachios and rose petals. let it sit for
   at least 5 minutes — it tightens up beautifully as it cools.

## notes from making this

- if you forget to bloom the saffron and just toss it in dry, you'll get
  flecks instead of color. still good. just less golden.
- the cardamom is *non-negotiable* for me. the rosewater is.
- leftovers go thick in the fridge — loosen with a splash of milk before
  reheating gently on the stovetop.

eat warm, slowly, with a small spoon.
